Orphan Soup (serves 4–6 depending on how thinly you stretch the backstory)

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Ingredients

  • 1 medium orphan (freshly expired preferred, ~45–60 kg works best)
  • 2½ liters "found family" stock (just tap water + tears, simmered with a whisper of false hope)
  • 3 large "why me" onions, roughly chopped while ugly-crying
  • 4 ribs celery nobody wanted
  • 6 "maybe next year" carrots, still slightly warm
  • 1 whole "you were supposed to save me" head of garlic, smashed
  • 2 bay leaves (the only things that stayed)
  • 1½ cups "it gets better" barley (spoiler: it doesn't)
  • 1 tbsp "you're so strong" salt (measure with the back of a sympathy card)
  • Freshly ground "thoughts and prayers" black pepper to taste
  • Optional garnish: one (1) small plush animal that smells faintly of hospital hand sanitizer

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Instructions

  1. Strip the orphan of anything that might remind people a person once lived inside it. (Clothing, name, unfinished homework, etc.) Set the identity aside—you won't need it again.
  2. Quarter the remains with the kind of dull knife social services gives foster parents. The resistance is part of the flavor.
  3. In your largest pot (bonus points if it's the one they used to heat up SpaghettiOs at 2 a.m. while waiting for someone to show up), sweat the onions in a film of orphan grease until they become translucent with regret.
  4. Add celery and carrots. Cook until they soften and start asking themselves what they did to deserve being here.
  5. Toss in the garlic and bay leaves. Stir while quietly repeating "it's not your fault" until you no longer believe it.
  6. Add the meaty bits. Let them brown just enough that the neighbors can smell something and decide not to call anyone.
  7. Pour in the stock. Bring to a simmer while thinking about every birthday that ended with "maybe next year."
  8. Add barley. Simmer uncovered for 45–60 minutes or until the grains have absorbed every last drop of optimism in the room.
  9. Taste. It should be somewhere between "mild disappointment" and "bone-deep futility." Adjust seasoning with more salt and existential dread.
  10. Ladle into bowls. Float a single plush ear or eye on top if you're feeling festive.
